Regular Check-Ups

Frequency: Schedule regular visits to your healthcare provider to monitor key health indicators like blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar levels. Depending on your health status, this could be annually or more frequently.

Importance: Regular check-ups help detect any potential problems early, making them easier to manage and treat before they become serious.

Know Your Numbers

Key Indicators: Keep track of important health metrics, including:

Blood Glucose Levels: High blood sugar levels can indicate prediabetes or diabetes. Monitoring helps in managing your diet and lifestyle accordingly.

Cholesterol Levels: High levels of LDL cholesterol (bad cholesterol) and low levels of HDL cholesterol (good cholesterol) increase the risk of heart disease.

Blood Pressure: High blood pressure (hypertension) is a major risk factor for heart disease and stroke. Knowing your numbers can help you take steps to lower it if necessary.

Screening Tests

HbA1c Test: This test measures your average blood glucose levels over the past 2-3 months. It helps in diagnosing diabetes and assessing how well diabetes is being managed if you have it.

Lipid Panel: A lipid panel measures cholesterol levels in your blood, including total cholesterol, LDL (bad) cholesterol, HDL (good) cholesterol, and triglycerides. It helps assess your risk of heart disease and guides lifestyle or medication adjustments.
