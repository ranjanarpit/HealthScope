Regular Exercise

150 Minutes of Moderate-Intensity Aerobic Activity: This includes activities that get your heart rate up and make you breathe harder, like brisk walking, cycling, swimming, or dancing. These activities should be spread throughout the week, such as 30 minutes a day, five days a week.

75 Minutes of Vigorous-Intensity Exercise: Vigorous activities like running, fast cycling, or high-intensity interval training (HIIT) significantly increase your heart rate and breathing. This type of exercise can be done in shorter periods, such as 25 minutes three times a week.

Why It Matters: Regular aerobic exercise helps to improve cardiovascular health, increase insulin sensitivity (which helps in controlling blood sugar levels), and assist in weight management.

Strength Training

Twice a Week: Incorporating muscle-strengthening exercises at least twice a week is essential. This can include activities like weight lifting, resistance band exercises, body-weight exercises (push-ups, squats), or yoga.

Boosts Metabolism: Muscle-strengthening exercises help build lean muscle mass, which increases your metabolism, helping you burn more calories even at rest.

Improves Insulin Sensitivity: Strength training improves the body's ability to manage blood sugar levels, reducing the risk of type 2 diabetes.

Stay Active Throughout the Day

Avoid Long Periods of Sitting: Prolonged sitting is linked to an increased risk of chronic diseases, including heart disease and diabetes. Breaking up long periods of sitting with movement is crucial.

Move Regularly: Take short breaks to stretch, walk around, or do light exercises. Incorporating activities like standing desks, short walks, or even stretching at your desk can make a big difference in maintaining your overall health.
